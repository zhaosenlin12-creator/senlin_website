# 14islands Reference Notes

Source: https://www.14islands.com/
Capture: `14islands-home.png`, `14islands-capture.json`

## What It Is

14islands is the strongest structural reference for the Senlin personal site. It reads as a premium design and technology agency rather than a novelty demo. The captured page exposes a real content system: top navigation, massive typographic hero, concise positioning copy, and a dense body of case studies.

## Transferable Ideas

- Use oversized typography as the main visual asset, not decoration. Their hero lets typography behave like architecture.
- Keep the navigation clear and calm. The site can be experimental while the wayfinding stays conventional.
- Make the main promise short and business-readable. 14islands states its value in one sentence before showing work.
- Present projects as a curated portfolio, not generic cards. Their case labels combine client/category, which can translate into project/category tags for Senlin's apps, teaching scenes, and competition results.
- Prefer generous empty space over filling every viewport with effects. The negative space is part of the premium feeling.

## What Not To Copy

- Do not copy their exact typography layout, logo treatment, text, or assets.
- Do not make Senlin's whole site light gray. His teaching/robotics identity can keep a darker stage, but with 14islands-level spacing and hierarchy.
- Do not overdo the agency framing. Senlin is a person and educator, not a studio.

## Senlin Translation

- Replace "Design & Technology" with a large identity statement such as "教育 / 代码 / 机器人" or "Teaching / Code / Robotics".
- Turn app and teaching achievements into case-study rows: `Python 冒险岛 / 游戏化编程`, `课堂反馈系统 / 教学服务闭环`, `WRCC / 机器人竞赛成果`.
- Use calm top navigation: `人物`, `作品`, `课堂`, `成果`, `联系`.
- Use a light/dark contrast rhythm: dark immersive hero, warm off-white project sections, dark gallery finale.
