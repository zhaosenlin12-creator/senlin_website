# Active Theory Reference Notes

Source: https://activetheory.net/
Capture: `active-theory-home.png`, `active-theory-capture.json`

## What It Is

Active Theory is the atmospheric and technical reference. The captured page is almost entirely canvas-driven, with very little DOM text. That tells us the site relies on a runtime scene, motion, sound toggle, and sparse navigation rather than traditional layout.

## Transferable Ideas

- Use darkness as a stage, not as a theme color. The black field makes small cyan marks feel precise and intentional.
- Let the first seconds feel like entering a system. A loader, scan, or orbit motif can create anticipation.
- Keep visible UI sparse during immersive moments: only essential navigation, audio/state controls, or minimal labels.
- Make the technology feel alive through motion: particles, field lines, pointer-reactive depth, subtle temporal noise.
- Use canvas for atmosphere, not for all content. Their approach works for a creative technology studio, but Senlin still needs readable proof and trust.

## What Not To Copy

- Do not make the whole page canvas-only. Parents and institutions need scannable content.
- Do not require audio or heavy GPU effects for the core message.
- Do not hide the site behind a long loader.

## Senlin Translation

- Add a lightweight canvas/Three-style "learning field" in the hero: orbital dots, lesson nodes, robot-path traces, or code constellations.
- Keep it low-contrast and refined: cyan, blue-white, and one warm accent.
- Use a short boot sequence for first impression, but make content visible quickly.
- Use pointer movement and scroll position to gently shift the field, giving the feeling of a live educational technology system.
